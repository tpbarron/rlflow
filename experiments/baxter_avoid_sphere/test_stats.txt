* Data for test runs for no training, and then 25, 50, 100, 150, 200, 250, and 500 iterations of finite difference gradient descent. Each gradient estimation used 2*num weights variations which in this case was 2*14 (ie: 2 * num_bases * num_dof, only 2 basis function where used and the dof of the arm is 7). Each weight variation was drawn from a gaussian distribution where the mean was 0 and the stdev was 0.1*(stdev of existing weights). The learning rate was set to a static .01
* Each run was 250 timesteps, so at the beginning the arm passed through the sphere for about 1/6th of the time
* The reward function = .5 * exp(-dist) + .5 * exp(-(steps in space)/timesteps); the optimal reward is 1. The numbers after steps in sphere are reward for distance and reward for not being in the sphere
* The sphere had radius of .05, essentially a 5 cm radius sphere in gazebo. I positioned the sphere at the (x,y,z) coordinate of the baxter::left_upper_forearm when it was half way through the initial trajectory (125 timesteps).
